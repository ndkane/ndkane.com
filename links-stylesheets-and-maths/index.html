At the end of the last lesson, I’d made ndkane.com a real, live, website. I was very proud that I hadn’t given up, or exploded into a big neurotic mess at the task of learning about getting it out in the open. As Aral demonstrated, you don’t have to know everything off by heart, there’s (almost) always a simple, step-by-step introduction to anything. Of course, as also mentioned by Aral, it would be nice if it was a one-click operation, but you can’t win every time.

The next task involved the merits of good typographical practise. Before this lesson I had absolutely no idea that there was an ‘em’ dash, what a ‘hyper minus’ was, or that there was a difference between quotation marks. I definitely didn’t realise how important they are in web development, particularly once you have noticed the difference. Paying attention to these nuances are the difference between a quality website and an average one. Aral emphasised the importance of considering this, as it shows a measure of integrity and attention to detail that is really valued in web development. It reads with more fluidity, and much more naturally. I learnt that if these behaviours are identified at the very beginning, when creating content, it creates a much better attitude to bring to web development in general.
